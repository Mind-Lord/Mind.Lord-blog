In a world of bosses, we need leaders

Being a boss is easy, just be ruthless with your employees in every way possible, but no one will feel bad when your time has come.
While being a leader is harder but it's much more rewarding, learn to be an example for your employees and make them feel important to you.
There will be huge benefits for great leaders, now you have to choose which one you want to become.

1. What's a Leader

I would like to start by saying that this is my personal opinion and how I would act as a leader.
Everyone has their own definition of leadership, and here I'm going to try to explain mine.
A leader is at the top thanks to his charisma and the ability to inspire others to follow him.
He isn't the best at everything but can overcome anything with the support of his teammates.
The leader I want to become is someone who helps his teammates achieve their goals and inspires them to improve themselves. "Leader" is a fantastic word not solely because of what it means.
It's very hard to become a leader, but the reward of becoming one is the journey itself.
Becoming a leader means studying real leaders and practicing with real people, not just puppets.

2. How to manage people

There are many types of people, and you have to learn how to manage each one of them.
How? Simply by recognizing their type.
For example:

  1. High motivation, low skills.
  2. Low motivation, high skills.
  3. High motivation, high skills.
  4. Low motivation, low skills.
You may think, "I need only the third type of people, and I'm done."
It's the easiest thing to think, and you're right, but you must at least learn to improve the first two types of people, and it isn't that simple.
The first type, "High motivation, low skills," needs to gain experience.
You can pair them with the second type, "Low motivation, high skills,"
but you have to do more for the second type because just seeing a motivated person won't completely help them.
What you have to do is give them challenges and let them inspire you by giving them carte blanche*.
Let them work on a project they like; they may surprise you in the end.
The third type, "High motivation, high skills," is like a divine blessing. I hope you can find them because it's complicated.
You have to keep them as long as possible; no one knows what they really think about, so watch out.

*complete freedom to act as one wishes.

3. Low motivation, low skills.

This type needs a full paragraph.
"Low motivation, low skills" are people who, due to their lack of skill, lose motivation.
They don't need empathy; they need to be involved in group projects more often and need easy to medium difficulty tasks.
Once their work becomes recognized, they'll start to try to do more and improve themselves on their own.
They need approval and a great push to start. You don't have to be too aggressive, or they'll quit.
In my opinion, this type can become the third more easily than the first two combined, but they need a bit of time and effort to get there.

4. Everyone can become a leader

You can become a leader, but if you don't want to, that's absolutely fine.
A leader has a lot of responsibilities, and if you don't like having pressure on yourself, it's better not to be a leader.
However, becoming a leader can be very satisfying, and if you can overcome your own fears,
you would already be a better leader than one who is born with that skill.
Even in another context, Tom "The Quadfather" Platz said:
"I'll not be a victim of my genetics; my genetics will be a victim to me."
All you have to do is choose between being a follower or a leader.
